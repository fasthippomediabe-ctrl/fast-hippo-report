4. Photo Specifications & Best Practices (from Observed Patterns)

Specification What Competitors Do What Fast Hippo Should Do
Image quality Osky Blue uses a professional photographer (Christopher Munn). Seota has high-quality shots. Kinetic Curve uses phone-quality candid shots. Hire a photographer for initial exterior/interior session (2-hour shoot, ~$300-500). Use phone for ongoing candid and portfolio shots.
Resolution Google recommends 720px minimum. All competitors meet this. Upload at 1200px+ width minimum. JPG format, under 5MB per image.
File naming Not directly observable, but Google uses EXIF data for geo-relevance. Name files descriptively: "fast-hippo-media-frisco-office-exterior.jpg" — Google can read filenames.
Geo-tagging Osky Blue and Seota photos are tied to Frisco office parks that Google Maps already knows. Take photos at 7700 Preston Rd with location services ON. The GPS coordinates in EXIF data reinforce your Frisco address.
Cover photo Osky Blue: Professional building exterior at dusk. Seota: Aerial building shot. Kinetic Curve: Interior with people. Upload your best exterior shot first — Google often uses the first owner-uploaded photo as the cover. A professional exterior shot of 7700 Preston Rd is your cover photo target.
Video format Seota uploads 23-45 second clips. Short, not polished — they're functional, not cinematic. 30-60 seconds max. MP4 format. Landscape orientation. No intro/outro needed — just straightforward office tours, quick tips, or team introductions.

6. Implementation Priority — Start Here

1

Book a 2-hour professional photo shoot at 7700 Preston Rd this week.

Get exterior shots at two times of day (daylight + golden hour), interior workspace shots, conference room, and individual/team headshots. Osky Blue's single professional exterior photo by Christopher Munn is their entire GBP photo identity — and they rank #1. One session gives you your foundation of 10-15 professional photos.

2

Upload 5 photos in Week 1 — exteriors and interiors first.

Google uses the first owner-uploaded photos to establish the "cover" image and populate the photo categories. Get your building exterior up first (this is what Osky Blue and Seota both lead with), followed by interiors. This immediately triggers the "Inside" category tab on your GBP.

3

Start capturing portfolio screenshots from every project — now.

Seota's photo library is heavily populated with client website screenshots. These serve double duty: (1) they demonstrate your work to prospects who browse your GBP photos, and (2) they add volume to your photo count without needing photoshoots. Every time a site launches or a campaign hits a milestone, take a clean screenshot. This is your ongoing content engine — no competitors besides Seota are doing this consistently.

4

Record your first 30-second GBP video in Month 2.

Seota is the only competitor with a dedicated "Videos" tab in their GBP. Their clips are 23-45 seconds — not polished productions. Walk through your office on your phone saying "Welcome to Fast Hippo Media in Frisco, Texas — here's where we build SEO, AEO, and digital marketing strategies for local businesses." Upload it. You'll be one of two businesses in this competitive set with video content.

5

Ensure every photo is taken at your office with GPS enabled.

Google reads EXIF GPS data from photos. When you upload a photo taken at 7700 Preston Rd, Frisco, TX, the embedded coordinates reinforce your address to Google's local algorithm. Both Osky Blue and Seota are in well-known Frisco office parks that Google Maps already geo-indexes heavily. Taking photos at your physical office with location services on is a free geo-relevance signal.
