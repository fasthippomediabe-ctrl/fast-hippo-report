Key Finding: Osky Blue ranks #1 in the Map Pack with ZERO posts in 4+ years. This proves that GBP posts alone don't determine rankings — reviews (133), category match, and address proximity matter far more. However, Seota's consistent posting correlates with maintaining relevance despite a mismatched primary category. The takeaway: posting is a supporting signal, not a primary lever. Your posting plan should be sustainable, not exhausting.
Avoid Kinetic Curve's Approach: Posting 3–5 times per day with repetitive content, aggressive hashtags (#success #money #motivation), and fear-based copy is a social media tactic, not a GBP optimization strategy. Google has stated that GBP post frequency has minimal direct ranking impact. Over-posting with recycled content risks appearing spammy and may trigger quality filters. It also doesn't align with Fast Hippo Media's premium brand positioning.

3. Fast Hippo Media — GBP Posting Plan

Posting Cadence: 6–8 Posts Per Month (2 per week)

This cadence outpaces Seota (1–2/mo), avoids Kinetic Curve's spam-like over-posting (90–150/mo), and is sustainable for a small agency team. Google's own guidance indicates that weekly posts keep your profile "fresh" without diminishing returns.

5. Posting Rules — What to Do and What to Avoid

Do This (Observed in Top Performers) Avoid This (Observed in Lower Performers)
Use multi-image posts (2–4 images per post) — Seota does this on every case study Single generic stock photos with no branding
Include city names naturally in copy: "Frisco," "Plano," "DFW," "North Texas" Keyword stuffing or unnatural location mentions
Use "Learn more" or "Book online" as your CTA — the only CTAs that work in GBP "Click the link in our BIO" — copied from Instagram, doesn't function in GBP
Write original, long-form copy (100–200 words per post, like Seota) Short 1-sentence posts or copy-pasted social captions
Brand every image with Fast Hippo logo and consistent colors Unbranded images that could belong to anyone
Post consistently (2x/week) — sustainability beats bursts Posting 5 times in one day then going silent for weeks
Use the Offer post type for lead gen (it displays differently in GBP with dates + CTA button) Putting all posts as "Updates" even when an offer would be more effective
Mention specific results: traffic numbers, ranking improvements, conversion rates Vague claims: "We helped them grow" without any specifics
Keep a professional, expert tone aligned with your brand Emoji-heavy, hashtag-loaded, motivational-quote-style posts

6. Implementation Priority — Start Here

1

Publish your first case study post this week.

Pick your best recent client project. Use Template A. Include the client's city, their industry, what you delivered, and one measurable result. Add 2–3 images (website screenshot, before/after, results chart). This single post type is what Seota builds 70% of their GBP presence around — because it works.

2

Publish your first AEO thought leadership post.

No competitor is owning the AEO narrative in GBP posts. Seota touched on it once (Aug 2025 AI/SEO post). You should own this space entirely. Use Template B to create a post about Answer Engine Optimization — this is your unique differentiator and no one in the Frisco market is talking about it in their GBP.

3

Create a standing Offer post (refreshed monthly).

Use the GBP Offer post type (not Update) for a "Free Digital Presence Audit" or "Free AEO Visibility Check." This displays as a special card in your GBP with a start/end date and prominent CTA button. Kinetic Curve uses lead gen offers aggressively — adopt the concept, but execute it with the quality and professionalism of your brand.

4

Set a recurring schedule: Tuesday + Thursday posting.

Consistency signals an active, engaged business. Block 30 minutes on Tuesdays and Thursdays to draft and publish GBP posts. Rotate through the 5 post types on the calendar above. After 4 weeks, you'll have 8 posts establishing a visible, professional GBP presence that surpasses Seota's and far exceeds Osky Blue's.

5

Create a branded image template set.

Every competitor with active posting uses consistent branded graphics. Create 5 Canva/Figma templates: (1) Case Study card, (2) AEO Insight card, (3) Offer announcement, (4) Testimonial quote card, (5) Community/seasonal card. All should use Fast Hippo colors, logo, and the same layout style. This makes posting faster and builds visual brand recognition in the GBP panel.

Remember: GBP posting is a supporting signal — not a silver bullet. The data shows that Osky Blue ranks #1 with zero posts but 133 reviews. Your biggest ranking lever is still review generation (you have 15 vs. their 133). Use these posts to reinforce your expertise and drive conversions, but continue prioritizing review acquisition as the #1 ranking lever identified in the previous analysis.
