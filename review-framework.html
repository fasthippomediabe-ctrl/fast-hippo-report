How to Prompt Keywords Without Scripting Reviews

Review Request Email/Text — Keyword Prompt Method
Hi [CLIENT NAME], Thank you for trusting Fast Hippo Media with your [SERVICE: SEO / web design / PPC / AEO strategy]. If you have a moment, we'd really appreciate a Google review sharing your experience. A few things that help other Frisco businesses find us: — What service we provided (e.g., [SEO, website redesign, Google Ads, AEO]) — What results or outcomes you noticed — Anything about working with our team Here's the direct link: [GOOGLE REVIEW LINK] Thanks so much, [YOUR NAME], Fast Hippo Media
Important: Never script or pre-write reviews for clients — this violates Google's policies and can result in review removal or GBP suspension. The prompt above works by reminding clients what to talk about (service type, results, team), which naturally generates the keyword-rich language you need. This is the same approach that produces Seota's "wordpress," "shopify," and "web development" keyword tags organically.

5. Review Acquisition Channels — Ranked by Observed Competitor Success

1

Post-Project Completion Request (Seota's Primary Source)

Every Seota review references a specific completed project: "custom Shopify site," "website redesign," "WordPress build." Send a review request within 48 hours of project delivery or a measurable milestone (traffic increase, site launch, first leads from a campaign). This is your highest-conversion moment.

Trigger: Website goes live, first month SEO report shows improvement, PPC campaign hits target ROAS, or AEO audit delivered.

2

Consultation/Strategy Session Attendees (Kinetic Curve's Primary Source)

Kinetic Curve generates ~50% of reviews from people who attended a consultation, podcast recording, or strategy session — not just paying clients. If you offer free audits, AEO assessments, or discovery calls, ask for a review afterward. The review mentions the experience, which generates "informative," "strategy," and "insight" keywords naturally.

Trigger: After any free audit, discovery call, AEO visibility check, or speaking engagement.

3

Long-Term Client Check-In (Osky Blue's Client Retention Pattern)

Patrick Cline's review of Osky Blue mentions "for seven years, they have always been professional." Long-term clients who haven't reviewed yet are low-hanging fruit. Send a personalized request to any client you've served for 6+ months who hasn't left a review.

Trigger: 6-month or 12-month client anniversary, quarterly business review, contract renewal.

4

Event/Workshop Attendees (Osky Blue's Bulk Source)

~70+ of Osky Blue's 133 reviews came from seminar/training attendees over a concentrated period. If you run any workshops, webinars, Frisco Chamber events, or speaking engagements, request reviews from attendees. This is a volume play — even non-clients can leave reviews about the educational value.

Trigger: Post-event follow-up email within 24 hours of any public event or webinar.

7. Reply Execution Checklist — For Every Review

✓ Requirement Why (Observed From Competitors)
□ Reply within 48 hours Kinetic Curve replies within days. Seota's most recent reply was within hours. Osky Blue's months-late replies show disengagement.
□ Use the reviewer's first name All three competitors do this consistently — "Thank you, Shane!" / "Andreh, it is always an honor"
□ Name the client's business Kinetic Curve does this every time ("Samurai Jiu-Jitsu"). Seota and Osky Blue miss this opportunity entirely.
□ Echo back the service mentioned If the reviewer mentions "website," reply with "your new website." If they mention "SEO," echo "your SEO campaign." Reinforces the keyword signal.
□ Include "Frisco" or "DFW" once Kinetic Curve embeds "Prosper, Texas" in replies. Neither Seota nor Osky Blue do this — it's a missed geo-signal opportunity.

□ End with a forward-looking statement Kinetic Curve: "Looking forward to your incredible growth." Seota: "Thanks again for being a great client." A forward statement signals an active, ongoing relationship.
□ Keep it 2-4 sentences (not 1, not 6) Seota's 1-sentence replies miss keyword opportunities. Kinetic Curve's 5+ sentence replies can feel excessive. 2-4 sentences is the sweet spot.
