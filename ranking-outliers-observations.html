Observation — Factor Interactions
The data shows two distinct paths to a top-3 position in this dataset:

Path observed at #1: Massive review volume (133) overrides a partial category match and the absence of all other signals (no keywords, no posting activity). Review volume at this scale appears to function as a standalone dominant signal.

Path observed at #2 and #3: Exact primary category match ("Marketing agency") enables top-3 ranking even with moderate (#2, 46 reviews) or minimal (#3, 7 reviews) review volume. Category relevance appears to function as a multiplier that amplifies the effect of other signals — or compensates for their absence.

Path observed at #6: High review volume (48), maximum profile completeness (71+ photos, active posting, rich descriptions), and 15+ years of tenure cannot compensate for a weak primary category match ("Website designer"). This combination produces only a #6 ranking.

No business in this dataset ranks in the top 3 with both a weak category match and fewer than 100 reviews.
